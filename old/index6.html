Team History- Our Story

 

The Huntington High School Robotics Team officially came into existence upon registering for the FRC in December 2013. Preliminary discussion about the formation of a robotics club or team began long before the team became official in November of 2012, between then sophomore Joe Saginaw and junior Nick Sherman. Joe had attended the FRC SBPLI regional competition at Hofstra University for the 2012 and 2013 games, Rebound Rumble and Ultimate Ascent, respectively, as a spectator. In December, Joe first approached Judyann Pazienza, a physics teacher at Huntington High School, about advising and mentoring the new team if it was to be formed. Her initial reaction to this was largely positive and Joe and Ms. Pazienza met with the science chairperson, Mrs. Rae Montesano, about forming this club. During this time Joe and Nick became more aware of the other robotics teams in Huntington and surrounding communities, including the Walt Whitman robotics team, FRC team 2161 (South Huntington School District). Before their visit, Ms. Pazienza, Joe, and Nick had only considered participating in cheaper and less resource intensive competitions than the FRC, however after discussing with Team 2161, they discovered it had been formed with limited  resources but managed to have a successful first season. For that reason, this can be seen as a critical point in the teams history as registering for the FRC was then seriously considered and was then viewed as feasible for the first time since the concept of the team had been discussed The next month, Ms. Pazienza or Mrs. Montesano became in contact with the Rotary Club of Huntington, and a meeting was arranged with them. The meeting occurred in June of 2013, and the attendees included Joe, Mrs. Montesano, Mrs. Pazienza, science teacher Mrs. Lori Kenny, Rotary Club President Mrs. MJ Fitzgerald, and her husband, Mr. Greg Fitzgerald who judged the FRC SBPLI regional. The meeting helped gain funding from the Rotary Club and gave the team’s leaders direction with respect to the future of the team. The Rotary Club also offered to commit funds to begin an FLL team at Finley Middle School, and to send a student to a FIRST Robotics summer camp for one week at Dowling College. Later that summer, Mrs. Pazienza arranged for a meeting with friends of hers she knew from her engineering career at various companies, including Globecomm Systems Inc. and Spectrum Thin Films. A visit to FESTO’s mobile mechatronics lab was also arranged, and Joe, Justin (Mrs. Pazienza’s son), Mrs. Pazienza, and another student, John Toprak, visited these companies and the lab in July. A follow up email was sent to each company and FESTO offered to donate parts to the team, although this offer was never taken advantage of for various reasons. At the end of July, Nick attended the FIRST Robotics summer camp at Dowling college from July 22nd through  26th. Joe also attended a summer camp at Worcester Polytechnic Institute in Massachusetts for ten days. Both these camps were meant to teach campers about robotics, FIRST robotics in particular, and prepare them for leading or being a member of an FRC team. In August of 2013 plans for the coming school year were laid out and the future of the team was discussed. A date for a robotics club interest meeting was set for September, and another meeting was arranged with an engineering firm in town, RMS Engineering. Joe, Nick, and Mrs. Pazienza met with one of the partners of the company, Mr. Wayne Muller, and an employee, Mr. Aaron Machtay. Mr. Machtay was a former student of Huntington High School and had limited robotics experience in college at Stony Brook University. Working at a civil engineering firm, Mr. Muller was unable to offer the team mentorship, but graciously offered to print any flyers, posters, or paper products the team might need. The first official meeting of the robotics club was held in September of the 2013-2014 school year, and close to 50 students attended the meeting. For their first meeting, Joe and Ms. Pazienza were ecstatic at this turn out, but many students did not realize the commitment that being a member entailed. A mailing list in addition to a Facebook group were created to facilitate contact between Joe and the team. Around this time, Ms. Pazienza was contacted by Mr. Mark McLeod, who was a mentor for the Hauppauge robotics team, FRC team 358. Mr. McLeod was a retired programmer who was a prominent figure in the FIRST community, and offered to come train the rookie Huntington team. His offer was accepted and he held 3 or 4 training sessions at Huntington High School, teaching members of the team basic mechanical and electrical engineering skills, and how to set  up the various systems that allow the robot to function. The team had its first fundraiser, a bake sale, before school on Halloween morning.
