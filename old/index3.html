Finance & Fundraisers


The Huntington High School robotics team deals with a significant amount of money every year that is allocated for different purposes.

Sources of Funding

Applying for grants and other similar financial endowments

Directly contacting potential sponsors about donating money or goods and services to the robotics team

Having fundraising events such as bake sales and raffles


Use of Funding

The robotics team’s funds will be used for numerous purposes. The usage of funds should always be for team purposes.

Competition fees: Funds will be used each year to register for competitions in which the team wants to
participate. The FRC may or may not continue to be a competition the team registers for annually.
Transportation: Transportation to and from competitions, events, and activities is a valid use of funds and may be necessary if transportation is not paid for by another organization such as the school. Busing may be required for larger events, but smaller vehicles may be rented as well. Team funded transportation may not be necessary if student members or the parents of members offer to drive members to or from competitions, events, or activities.
Materials: Funds will be used each year to buy materials to build the robot. Materials can also be purchased to improve the pit or build area.
Tools: Funds will be used when necessary to purchase tools, whether they be hand tools or electric tools, and to improve the workshop and build environment. “Tools” here means anything that facilitates the building of the robot and operation of the team.
Marketing: Funds will be used to purchase marketing materials, which will aid the marketing and public relations team in creating and maintaining sponsor relationships, promoting the team, and running events.
Membership expenses: Funds will be used to support team members and buy basic essentials (ex: snacks).
Special Projects: Funds will be used when members working on special projects that are not directly related to robotics but may be related to the team work area or the team’s efficiency need to purchase items to complete their project.

Budget

A comprehensive budget will be drafted every year by the team treasurer, during the summer before the new year begins or in the first few months of the school year. Prospective financial data will be assessed before the budget is written, and the budget will utilize the prospective financial data to best account for growth, continuity, or reduction of the team in any way. The team budget must be ratified by two-thirds of the team’s members in attendance at the budget ratification meeting. In order for the budget to take effect, it must be ratified and signed by the team captain, and the team vice-captain.
